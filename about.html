About Lumber River Native Plants

Leading the way in native plant conservation and wetland restoration

Our Story

Founded by Jep Whitlock over two decades ago, Lumber River Native Plants began as a passion project to preserve and promote the native plant species of the Lumber River region in North Carolina. What started as a small nursery has grown into the #1 native plant nursery in the Midwest and Southeast.

Our nursery is strategically located in Gibson, NC, at 7000 Livingston Rd, where we cultivate and propagate native plants specifically adapted to the unique conditions of the Lumber River watershed and surrounding areas.

Today, we serve customers across the Midwest and Southeast, providing high-quality native plants for residential landscaping, commercial projects, wetland restoration, and conservation initiatives.

Our Mission & Values

Native Plant Conservation

We are committed to preserving and promoting native plant species that are essential to local ecosystems and biodiversity.

Wetland Restoration

Specializing in aquatic plants and wetland species to support water quality improvement and habitat restoration projects.

Education & Outreach

Providing expert guidance and education to help customers make informed decisions about native plant selection and care.

Community Partnership

Working with local organizations, government agencies, and conservation groups to promote native plant awareness and conservation.

Sustainable Practices

Using environmentally responsible growing practices and promoting sustainable landscaping solutions.

Quality Assurance

Ensuring every plant meets our high standards for health, vigor, and genetic authenticity.

Meet Jep Whitlock

Owner, Founder & Lead Plant Specialist

Jep Whitlock brings over two decades of experience in native plant cultivation, wetland restoration, and conservation biology to Lumber River Native Plants. His deep knowledge of the Lumber River ecosystem and its native flora has made him a recognized expert in the field.

Jep's expertise includes:

  • Aquatic plant selection and cultivation
  • Wetland ecosystem restoration
  • Native hibiscus hybridization and propagation
  • Regional plant adaptation and hardiness
  • Conservation planning and implementation

When not at the nursery, Jep works with local conservation organizations and participates in wetland restoration projects throughout the Southeast.

Our Nursery Facilities

Propagation Greenhouse

Climate-controlled environment for starting native plants from seed and cuttings, ensuring optimal growing conditions.

Aquatic Plant Ponds

Specialized ponds and water features for growing aquatic and emergent wetland plants in their natural environment.

Native Plant Beds

Extensive field production areas where plants mature in natural soil conditions, developing strong root systems.

Delivery Fleet

Specialized vehicles equipped to safely transport plants throughout the Midwest and Southeast regions.

Certifications & Partnerships

  • North Carolina Native Plant Society - Active member and plant supplier
  • USDA Plant Materials Center - Partner in native plant research and development
  • North Carolina Department of Agriculture - Licensed nursery operation
  • Wetland Restoration Network - Certified supplier for restoration projects
  • Native Plant Trust - Supporting member and seed bank contributor
